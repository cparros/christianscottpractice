Christian Scott

A Trumpet Player Unlike Any Other.

Christian Scott is a 37 year old musician from New Orleans, Louisiana. He is best known for his Trumpet, Cornet, Flugelhorn, and Saprano Trombone playing abilities. Some of his most recent accomplishments include winning the JazzFM Innovator of the year award in 2016 as well as a grammy nomination. One little known fact about him is he is a third generation cheiftain in the Afro New Orleanian Masking Tribes aka Black Indians.

Bio: Early Life and Career

Christian Scott was born March 31, 1983 along with his twin brother Kiel Scott. His love of music began when he was 12 when his uncle, Donald Harrison Jr. , an alto saxophonist, began to teach him. Over the next 2 years he progressed exponentially and at the young age of 14 was accepted into the New Orleans Center for the Creative Arts (NOCCA)! Two years later he began playing in his uncles quintet where he also began recording music. After graduating from NOCCA Christian was awarded a scholarship to Berkely College of Music in Boston from which he graduated in 2004. While there his focus was a major in Professional music and a minor in film scoring.

Christians major label debut came in 2006 with Concord Records and his album "Rewind That". The album recieved a Grammy Nomination for the Best Contemporary Jazz Album. His second award opportunity came in 2010 with his album "Yesterday You Said Tomorrow" and it recieved the Edison Award. Since his debut in 2006 Christian has released 11 Albums and been a featured artist on many projects.

Review: My Quick Thoughts on Christian Scott

I stumbled onto Christian Scott back in 2006 when I heard his first album "Rewind That". I was a young drummer in the music section in Barnes and Nobles looking for new music to play to, and was a big fan of a drummer named Thomas Pridgen who happened to be the drummer for Christian's first album. Once I heard the first song I was hooked and couldn't stop listening to it. I had the Album on repeat. Since then I have been a non stop listener. In recent years he has added to his name. He is also now known as "aTunde Adjuah", to honor his West African self identity. He has also been heavily influenced by african rhythm and encorperated it into his recent projects which has made for very different yet captivating music. I am always excited to hear what he creates and cannot wait for his next album. If you are looking for new music in a world where many of the mainstream artists can sound the same I highly recommend giving Christian Scott a listen!

His newest album is called "Axiom" and can be streamed on most music streaming platforms.
